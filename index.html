Difficult airway algorithm
ASA 2022 guidelines
▾
Step 1 — assess before every case
Mallampati III/IV, thyromental distance <6cm, sternomental <12cm
Neck circumference >40cm, limited mouth opening, limited neck mobility
History of difficult airway, stridor, neck mass, radiation, obesity
Awake intubation — consider when:
Expected difficult intubation PLUS any of: difficult ventilation, aspiration risk, cannot tolerate apnea, difficult emergency invasive airway
Techniques: flexible bronchoscope, videolaryngoscopy, DL, retrograde wire
Optimize O2 throughout — HFNC 30-70 L/min, head elevated, apneic oxygenation
After induction — unanticipated difficult airway
▶ Call for help immediately
▶ Start with videolaryngoscope (preferred over DL)
▶ Can't see cords → bougie + VL → hyperangulated blade → flexible scope
▶ Limit attempts (3 + 1 by more experienced provider)
▶ Watch elapsed time and SpO2 constantly
Can ventilate / cannot intubate
Non-emergency pathway: consider awakening patient, SGA, alternative technique
Limit attempts — fixation on intubation wastes time
Cannot intubate / Cannot oxygenate (CICO)
Emergency — call for help NOW
Surgical cricothyrotomy: scalpel → bougie → ETT (6.0)
Needle cric + pressure regulated device as bridge
Consider ECMO if invasive approach fails
Brain damage in 4 min, death in 4-6 min from anoxia — do not delay
Extubation of difficult airway
Place airway exchange catheter before extubating
Extubate awake, in optimal position, with team present
Document and communicate difficult airway to patient and chart

Transfusion therapy
High-yield thresholds and triggers
▾
PRBCs — 1 unit raises Hgb ~1 g/dL, Hct ~3%
Transfuse at Hgb <7 g/dL in most patients
Transfuse at Hgb <8 g/dL in cardiac surgery, symptomatic CAD
Hgb >10 g/dL — transfusion rarely indicated
Symptomatic anemia (SOB, chest pain, hemodynamic instability) overrides threshold
FFP — contains all coagulation factors
Dose: 10-15 mL/kg raises clotting factors ~15%
Transfuse for: active bleeding + coagulopathy, INR >1.5 with bleeding
Massive transfusion: give FFP:PRBC in 1:1 ratio
Warfarin reversal: prefer PCC over FFP (faster, less volume)
Platelets — 1 apheresis unit raises count ~30,000-60,000
Prophylactic: transfuse if <10,000 (stable) or <20,000 (febrile/unstable)
Pre-procedure: maintain >50,000
Active bleeding / neuro surgery: maintain >100,000
Do NOT transfuse in HIT or TTP — worsens thrombosis
Cryoprecipitate — fibrinogen + factor VIII + vWF + XIII
Transfuse when fibrinogen <150 mg/dL with bleeding (or <200 mg/dL in DIC)
10-unit pool raises fibrinogen ~70 mg/dL
Use for: massive hemorrhage, DIC, vWD, hemophilia A (if specific factor unavailable)
LAST — local anesthetic systemic toxicity
Signs: perioral numbness, tinnitus, metallic taste → seizures → cardiovascular collapse
Treatment: Intralipid 20% — bolus 1.5 mL/kg IV over 1 min, then infusion 0.25 mL/kg/min
Bupivacaine most cardiotoxic — avoid in Bier blocks

High-yield pearls
Amides (lidocaine, bupivacaine, ropivacaine) — metabolized by liver
Esters (chloroprocaine, tetracaine) — metabolized by plasma cholinesterase
Bupivacaine most cardiotoxic — high protein binding → hard to displace from cardiac Na channels
Ropivacaine — less cardiotoxic alternative to bupivacaine for nerve blocks
Epi extends duration and reduces systemic absorption — avoid in end-arterial blocks (digits, penis, nose)
LAST signs: perioral numbness → tinnitus → seizures → cardiac arrest
LAST treatment: Intralipid 20% — 1.5 mL/kg bolus, then 0.25 mL/kg/min infusion
Methemoglobinemia risk: prilocaine, benzocaine — treat with methylene blue 1-2 mg/kg
