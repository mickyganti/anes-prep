High-yield pearls
Amides (lidocaine, bupivacaine, ropivacaine) — metabolized by liver
Esters (chloroprocaine, tetracaine) — metabolized by plasma cholinesterase
Bupivacaine most cardiotoxic — high protein binding → hard to displace from cardiac Na channels
Ropivacaine — less cardiotoxic alternative to bupivacaine for nerve blocks
Epi extends duration and reduces systemic absorption — avoid in end-arterial blocks (digits, penis, nose)
LAST signs: perioral numbness → tinnitus → seizures → cardiac arrest
LAST treatment: Intralipid 20% — 1.5 mL/kg bolus, then 0.25 mL/kg/min infusion
Methemoglobinemia risk: prilocaine, benzocaine — treat with methylene blue 1-2 mg/kg
